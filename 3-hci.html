b. HCI Principles

Visibility - All the actions that a user can make are clearly highlighted through buttons on the user interface. Everything is bundled up on the same page, therefore, there is no need to scroll down to access additional features.

Feedback - Users can clearly see when they have interacted through the interface. Once a document is uploaded, users can immediately see the uploaded document in the ‘my documents’ section. Moreover, when receiving a signature from an institution, users get notified that their document has been signed, while the document gets placed in a signed documents section.

Affordance - The interactive buttons and sections have been distinctively and suggestively named to reflect the action which occurs when the button is pressed. For example, users can click the sign up button to create an account or the login button to log into an existing account.

Mapping - Each action conducted on our website corresponds to its desired functionality. The user/institution portal has various controls that trigger different functionalities.

Constraints - Users as well as institutions are clearly guided through the set of actions they can make or not make on our website. Moreover, when a regular use creates an account, it has different capabilities than an institution account. While regular user accounts can upload and request documents to be signed, institutions can review those documents and choose to sign or not to sign them.

Consistency - Before designing our app, we have done extensive research on other similar applications that are already out there. As a result, we chose a theme that represents professionalism and reflects systems that have been created with a similar purpose. Consistency is also present in the ecosystem of our application, both users and institutions have similarly laid out pages and the visual queues are consistent throughout.
